1.1 General Theory — Web Scraping in Social Media Analytics
Social media analytics is the discipline of collecting, processing, and interpreting data generated by users
on social platforms such as Twitter/X, Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, Reddit, and YouTube. In the
modern digital economy, every like, share, comment, hashtag, and follower action constitutes a data point
that carries commercial, sociological, and reputational significance.
Why Web Scraping? Social media platforms expose limited data through official APIs — often
rate-limited, requiring approval, and restricted to recent data. Web scraping bridges this gap by
programmatically navigating web pages, identifying HTML structures, and extracting targeted content at
scale. Researchers, marketers, and data scientists use scraping to:
• Monitor brand mentions and sentiment trends in near real-time
• Collect competitor product reviews, pricing, and promotions
• Build datasets for training natural language processing (NLP) models
• Aggregate influencer performance metrics (engagement rate, follower growth)
• Track hashtag virality and geographic spread of topics
• Archive public posts for longitudinal academic research
From an analytics perspective, raw scraped data feeds into dashboards (Power BI, Tableau), network
graphs (Gephi), and sentiment engines. The quality of downstream analysis depends entirely on the
completeness and accuracy of extraction — making tools like Octoparse critical first-mile infrastructure in
any analytics pipeline.
1.2 About Octoparse
Octoparse is a cloud-based, no-code web scraping platform developed by Octopus Data Inc. It provides a
visual, point-and-click interface that allows users with zero programming knowledge to build sophisticated
data extraction workflows (called 'tasks'). It is widely used by marketing agencies, academic researchers,
e-commerce businesses, and journalists.

1.4 Step-by-Step Procedure
• Step 1: Install & Launch: Download Octoparse from octoparse.com. Install on Windows. Log in with
your free account credentials.
• Step 2: Create New Task: Click 'New Task' → Enter the URL of the target page (e.g., a Twitter/X
search results page, a LinkedIn company page, or a news site). Click 'Save URL'.
• Step 3: Auto-Detect Data: Octoparse's AI engine scans the page and highlights repeating data
patterns (e.g., post cards, product listings). Click 'Auto-detect webpage data'. Review the suggested
fields.
• Step 4: Customise Fields: In the Fields panel, rename auto-detected fields (e.g., Field1 →
post_text, Field2 → likes, Field3 → timestamp). Delete irrelevant fields. Add new fields by clicking
elements in the browser.
• Step 5: Handle Pagination: For multi-page data: click the 'Next Page' button on the page inside
Octoparse's browser → select 'Click to load next page' action. Set loop count (e.g., 50 pages).
• Step 6: Configure Loop for Cards: Click any repeating item (e.g., a tweet card) → Octoparse adds
a 'Loop Item' step. This ensures every card is iterated and all fields extracted from each.
• Step 7: Test Run (Local): Click 'Run' → select 'Run on your device'. Observe the browser
auto-scrolling and data populating in the preview table. Check for errors in field extraction.
• Step 8: Cloud Run & Schedule: Click 'Save Task' → 'Run in Cloud'. Optionally set a schedule (e.g.,
daily at 9 AM). Cloud run operates 24/7 without your computer.
• Step 9: Export Data: Once run completes → go to 'Extracted Data' → click 'Export' → choose CSV
or Excel. For Google Sheets integration, configure the API connector in Settings.
• Step 10: Verify & Clean: Open the exported file. Check for null values, duplicate rows, encoding
issues (especially for emojis or regional scripts). Use Excel or Python pandas for basic cleaning.

2.1 General Theory — Dashboards in Social Media Analytics

and data from multiple sources in a consolidated, real-time or near-real-time view. In social media
analytics, dashboards serve as command centres for:
• Monitoring follower growth, reach, impressions, and engagement rates across platforms
• Tracking campaign performance against benchmarks (CTR, conversion rate, cost-per-click)
• Identifying content that resonates most with target audiences
• Detecting sentiment shifts or brand crises early
• Comparing performance across time periods (week-on-week, month-on-month)
• Providing stakeholder-ready reports without requiring technical expertise
Power BI, developed by Microsoft, is an industry-leading business intelligence platform that transforms raw
data from hundreds of sources into interactive, shareable dashboards and reports. It is one of the most
widely used tools in corporate analytics environments.

7.1 General Theory — Social Network Analysis (SNA)
Social Network Analysis (SNA) is the methodological study of relationships and flows between people,
groups, organisations, or other entities. In social media analytics, SNA reveals:
• Who are the key influencers (high betweenness centrality nodes)?
• Which communities are forming around topics or brands (modularity/community detection)?
• How quickly does information diffuse through a network (path length analysis)?
• Which users form bridges between otherwise separate communities?
• What is the structural vulnerability of the network (removing which nodes disconnects it)?
• How does retweet/reply/mention topology differ from follower/following topology?
Graph Theory underpins SNA: a network is represented as G = (V, E) where V = vertices (nodes = users)
and E = edges (connections = mentions, retweets, follows). Directed graphs capture asymmetric
relationships (A retweets B, but B doesn't retweet A). Weighted edges encode strength (5 retweets
between A and B = heavier edge than 1 retweet).

8.1 General Theory — Hashtag Networks & Information Cascades
A hashtag network is a specialised type of social graph where nodes represent users who have used a
specific hashtag, and edges represent retweet, reply, or mention relationships among them. Analysing
hashtag networks reveals:
• Which users are central to spreading a trending topic (#campaign performance)
• Whether a hashtag is driven by organic conversation or coordinated bot activity
• Geographic distribution of conversation nodes
• Temporal evolution: how the network grows from a few seed users to viral scale
• Content theme clusters: different user groups discussing different aspects of the same hashtag
Information Cascade Theory explains how information spreads through a network in waves: a seed node
posts content, their followers see it and reshare, creating a second wave, and so on. In marketing
analytics, a deep, narrow cascade (long chain of single retweets) indicates viral organic spread; a wide,
shallow cascade (one node with thousands of direct retweets) indicates an influential hub broadcasting to
a passive audience.

Google Keyword Planner (ads.google.com/keywordplanner): Free with Google Ads account. Official
Google search volume data. Keyword ideas, search volume ranges, competition level, CPC estimates,

Ubersuggest (neilpatel.com/ubersuggest): Freemium. Enter keyword → shows volume, SEO difficulty,

Content ideas tab shows most-shared blog posts for the keyword.
Ahrefs Keywords Explorer: Premium. Most accurate clickstream-based volume data. Click-through rate
(CTR) estimation. Also known as (AKA) terms, questions, phrases match, having same terms. Parent topic
identification. SERP analysis.
SEMrush (semrush.com): Premium. Keyword Magic Tool: 25 billion keyword database. Broad, phrase,
exact, related, questions filters. Keyword difficulty, SERP features (Featured Snippet, People Also Ask).
Competitor keyword gap analysis. Organic positions tracker.
Google Trends (trends.google.com): Free. Real-time search trends. Compare up to 5 keywords.
Regional interest breakdown (state/district level in India). Related queries (rising = emerging trends).
Trending searches daily. YouTube search trends. Ideal for: detecting trending topics for social content
calendars.
Hashtagify (hashtagify.me): Hashtag-specific tool. Enter a hashtag → popularity score, correlation with
related hashtags, top influencers using it, trend over last 30 days. Distinguish between Instagram and
Twitter metrics. Paid for full features.
RiteTag (ritetag.com): Real-time hashtag grading. Paste your social media post → RiteTag colour-codes
each hashtag: Green = great reach now, Blue = great reach if used repeatedly, Red = overused, Black =
rarely searched. Browser extension for Instagram/Twitter.
Answer The Public (answerthepublic.com): Visualises keyword questions as a sunburst diagram. Enter
a topic → generates 100s of 'who, what, where, when, why, how' questions, prepositions, comparisons.

Keyword Tool (keywordtool.io): Generates long-tail keywords from Google Autocomplete, YouTube

TikTok Creative Centre — Keyword Insights: Free. TikTok-specific keyword trends for ad copy. Shows
which keywords are trending in TikTok ads by industry. Volume, CTR, impressions data for TikTok search

11.4 Step-by-Step Procedure
• Define Seed Keywords: Brainstorm 5–10 core terms directly related to your product/campaign. E.g.,
for an organic skincare brand: 'organic skincare', 'natural face cream', 'chemical-free moisturiser',
'vegan beauty India'.

• Google Keyword Planner Research: Open Keyword Planner → Discover new keywords → Enter
seed keywords → Set targeting: India, English → View results. Download CSV. Note: Volume,
Competition (Low/Medium/High), CPC. Sort by Volume descending. Identify top 20 keywords.
• Ubersuggest Keyword Ideas: Enter your top keyword (e.g., 'organic skincare India') → Keyword
Ideas → filter by Volume > 500, SEO Difficulty < 40. This is the 'low-hanging fruit' quadrant. Export to
CSV.
• Google Trends Comparison: Open Google Trends → Enter top 3 keywords → set: India, Last 12
months, Web Search → Compare trends. Identify which term is growing (rising trend) vs declining.
Switch to YouTube Search to see video content demand.
• Hashtag Research with Hashtagify: Enter brand keyword (e.g., 'organic') → view correlation map
→ identify top 20 related hashtags. Cross-reference with Instagram (search hashtag → note post
count: under 500K = niche, 500K–2M = moderate, 2M+ = oversaturated).
• RiteTag Real-Time Grading: Compose your social media post in Instagram/Twitter. Install RiteTag
extension → as you type hashtags, they turn green (ideal), blue (good for growth), or red (avoid). Aim
for a mix of green and blue hashtags.
• Answer The Public Questions Map: Enter your main keyword → download question map. Identify
'How to', 'What is', 'Best' questions — these become social media post topics, YouTube video ideas,
and blog content.
• Keyword Gap Analysis (SEMrush): SEMrush → Competitive Research → Keyword Gap → enter
your domain + 2 competitors. Filter: 'Missing' keywords (competitors rank, you don't). These are
priority content opportunities for social and SEO.
• Create Master Keyword Sheet: Compile all findings in Excel: Keyword/Hashtag | Platform | Monthly
Volume | Difficulty | CPC | Intent | Priority (High/Med/Low). Colour-code by priority. This becomes your
content strategy reference.
• Map Keywords to Content Calendar: Assign each high-priority keyword to a post type:
Informational → Blog/Infographic. Transactional → Product post. Question-based → Reel/YouTube
Short. Trending → Time-sensitive story/post. Build a 30-day content calendar based on
keyword-content mapping.
